USCO Project

Why USCO was designed?

Popular hardware platforms for IoT, such as Intel Edison, Arduino and Raspberry pi, provide GPIO interface. However, the supported voltage and current levels are typically limited (3.3V or 5V). Common industrial sensors and transducers use 24V digital IO which cannot be directly connected to these platforms without proper voltage conversion. The aim of this project was to develop universal voltage level converter shield for GPIO that allows the conversion between various digital IO voltage levels between 3.3V and 24V and voltage scaling for analog IO between 1.6 and 24V.

USCO Desing

USCO has two different modules for analog and digital voltage level converting needs as well as motherboard where these modules should be attached. The Analog Converter can transform analog signals between 1.6V and 24V or 1.6V and +/-12V.The Digital converter module has two separete bidirectional channels, which manages converting needs at one voltage intervals from 3.3V to 24V. Finally, the Motherboard supplies the modules with necessary power for operations and allows easier installation for the whole device. It requires ground and steady DC 5V supply to fuel the transformation processes as well as signals to be transformed.
